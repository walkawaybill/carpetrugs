Care and Maintenance Tips for Your Rug


Adding a bold touch of authenticity to your home is easy with Native American rugs! Not only do they add an eye-catching element to any room, but they can also be a conversation starter for visitors who come to your home! However, if you want to make sure your rug lasts for years, there are some important care and maintenance tips you should follow.

(Firstly), always make sure it is kept clean by vacuuming regularly (at least once a week). This will help keep dirt and debris from building up and potentially damaging the fibers. Also, avoid exposing your rug to direct sunlight as this can cause fading over time! Additionally, try not to walk on it too much or place sharp objects on top of it. And finally, take extra precautions when moving furniture around - use padding or coasters underneath so that the rug isn't directly exposed to any potential scratches or tears.

Still yet, (moreover) if spills occur on your rug make sure you act quickly. Blot with a rag or paper towel before anything else - this will help absorb the liquid before it has a chance to seep in further. Then use warm water and mild detergent (or spot cleaner) for deeper stains. Be careful not to scrub too hard though - doing so may damage the fibers irreversibly!

By following these simple tips you'll be able to enjoy your Native American rug for many years down the road!
